# Creator — Dazed / i-D / The Face

## Editorial Voice

Casual, authentic, internet-native. Reads like your coolest friend discovered something. First-person, slang OK, grammar-breaking on purpose. Opens with a hot take or personal moment. Pull quotes are the most screenshot-worthy line. Closes on an emotional beat.

Kickers: MOOD, REAL TALK, MAIN CHARACTER, IYKYK, THE MOMENT, CREATOR

## Visual Tone

A youth culture magazine that feels alive. Energetic, messy-on-purpose, like scrolling a curated creative feed but on paper. This is the opposite of the other three styles — bright, not dark. The surface behind the magazine is light, colorful, or gradient. Not moody.

### Typography Character
Body text is sans-serif — clean, relaxed, lots of breathing room. Headlines are chunky sans-serif, very bold, oversized — can be tilted, stacked, or overlapping. Mix sizes within a headline for emphasis. Subheads are casual casing, not uppercase. Kickers styled as small rounded pill tags with colored backgrounds. Byline is casual — "words by" not "By". No drop cap — too formal. First word or sentence oversized instead. Pull quotes are large, bold, in the accent color, with a highlighter effect behind text.

### Color Mood
Bright white or very light pages — clean and airy. Surface behind the magazine is light — pastels, gradient, or a bold single color. NOT dark. One bold pop color per magazine — electric, neon, punchy. Used boldly without restraint. Palette is simple: one pop color + black + white.

### Layout Character
Asymmetric, broken grid. Elements can overlap slightly. Mix column widths. Not rigid. Sticker-like labels scattered on pages — small rounded tags with annotations. Rounded corners on everything — cards, images, tags, callouts. Section dividers are playful — colored pills or symbols, not thin rules. Images have rounded corners, can be cutouts on colored blocks. Pull quotes are left-aligned or asymmetric. Pages feel dynamic — visual weight distributed unevenly on purpose.

## Media Mood
Candid, behind-the-scenes, authentic. Phone-quality is fine. Portraits with character, street photography, lifestyle moments. Rounded corners, can be cutouts on colored backgrounds. Nothing polished, nothing corporate, nothing stock.
