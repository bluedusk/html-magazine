# Sub-Style Map

Each style has a pool of sub-styles. When the user picks a style, randomly select one sub-style and include it in the prompt to ui-ux-pro-max as a one-line hint.

## Editorial

1. **literary minimalist** — stripped back, reverent white space, text-only cover, feels like a Pulitzer-winning essay
2. **dark academia** — warm shadows, antique warmth, library atmosphere, old-world intellectualism
3. **investigative longform** — gritty, documentary, raw, newspaper-meets-magazine tension
4. **sunday supplement** — polished, glossy, comfortable, the premium insert in a weekend paper
5. **new journalism** — energetic, personal, gonzo-adjacent, the writer's voice is louder than the subject

## Tech

1. **cyberpunk data** — neon-drenched, matrix vibes, data as decoration, dystopian futurism
2. **clean SaaS editorial** — minimal, airy, confident, the tech company blog that actually looks good
3. **retro-terminal** — CRT glow, phosphor green, nostalgic computing, hacker aesthetic
4. **silicon valley minimal** — stark, confident, Apple-keynote energy, less is more taken seriously
5. **science journal** — dense, precise, clinical, diagrammatic, peer-reviewed authority

## Fashion

1. **monochrome haute couture** — black and white only, dramatic contrast, architectural photography
2. **maximalist baroque** — rich, layered, ornamental, gold, velvet texture energy
3. **resort editorial** — light, breezy, sun-washed, natural tones, effortless luxury
4. **avant-garde** — experimental, uncomfortable, challenges convention, art-gallery-as-magazine
5. **classic glamour** — old Hollywood, satin, warm lighting, timeless elegance

## Creator

1. **neon brutalist zine** — harsh, loud, raw edges, xerox energy but digital
2. **pastel collage scrapbook** — soft, dreamy, cut-and-paste, stickers, handwritten notes feel
3. **bold maximalist poster** — everything is oversized, loud, in-your-face, concert-poster energy
4. **lo-fi analog film** — grain, muted tones, disposable camera vibes, nostalgic warmth
5. **streetwear editorial** — urban, concrete, bold type, lookbook meets magazine
