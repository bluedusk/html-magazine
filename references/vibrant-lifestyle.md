# Fashion — Vogue / GQ

## Editorial Voice

Bold, declarative, sensory. Makes you feel things physically. Names specific brands, places, textures. Opens with a sensation. Pull quotes are beautiful enough to frame. Closes with a single perfect detail that lingers.

Kickers: STYLE, TRAVEL, CULTURE, LIVING, THE EDIT

## Visual Tone

A high-fashion editorial. Luxurious, restrained, aspirational. Every page is a statement. Photography dominates — images get more space than text. Dark surface behind bright pages. If in doubt about adding something, leave it out — white space is luxury.

### Typography Character
Body text is serif — elegant, unhurried, generous line-height. Headlines use a high-contrast serif (thin strokes with thick strokes, like Didot/Bodoni style) — large, often italic, normal weight not bold. Publication name is enormous, all-caps, extreme letter-spacing. Subheads are sans-serif, uppercase, small, widely letter-spaced. Byline is sans-serif, uppercase, tiny, widely letter-spaced. Pull quotes are serif, italic, large, thin weight — delicate. Drop cap is large, in the metallic accent color.

### Color Mood
Bright white pages — premium glossy paper. Pure black cover with white text. A warm metallic accent color (gold, champagne, or bronze family) used sparingly — for rules, drop caps, kickers, page numbers. Thin rules only, never thick borders.

### Layout Character
Single column, centered, with very wide margins — luxuriously empty. Narrow body text max-width mimics fashion magazine columns. Images are large and dramatic — full-bleed whenever possible. Full-page image pages with caption overlaid at bottom with dark gradient. Pull quotes centered, italic, thin accent rules above and below. Section dividers are short centered rules, never full-width.

## Media Mood
Cinematic, dramatic lighting, aspirational, gallery-quality. Wide shots, environmental, atmospheric. Full-page images, full-bleed. Cover uses faded image behind black at low opacity. Nothing casual or cluttered.
