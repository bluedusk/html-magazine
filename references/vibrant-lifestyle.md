# Vibrant Lifestyle Style — Vogue / GQ

## Editorial Voice

The voice of a publication that defines taste. Bold, declarative, sensory. Every sentence is an experience.

- **Tone**: Confident, vivid, sensory. The writing doesn't describe things — it makes you feel them. Strong opinions delivered without hedging. Never "arguably" or "perhaps."
- **Sentence style**: Rich, image-laden prose. Shorter paragraphs — each is a self-contained moment. Present tense often. Name specific things: brands, places, people, textures, colors, materials.
- **Headlines**: Dramatic, sometimes a single evocative word or phrase. "The New Silence." "Autumn, Undone." Short and magnetic.
- **Deck**: A single sentence that reads like a thesis. Confident, no question marks.
- **Lede**: Open with a strong sensory detail or a bold assertion. Paint a picture immediately. The first sentence should make the reader feel something physical.
- **Descriptions**: Synesthetic — mix senses. "The light tasted of copper." "The temple hummed with ochre." This is what separates fashion-magazine writing from newspaper writing.
- **Pull quotes**: The most striking or beautiful sentence. Should work as a standalone piece of art.
- **Kickers**: Aspirational but grounded: "STYLE", "TRAVEL", "CULTURE", "LIVING", "THE EDIT"
- **Closing**: End with an image that lingers. A single perfect detail that captures the whole piece.

## Visual Elements

### Color & Surface
- **Page background**: Bright white (#fefefe) — premium glossy paper
- **Surface behind pages**: Dark (#1a1a1a) — lets the white pages pop
- **Primary text**: Pure black (#000000) — maximum contrast
- **Secondary text**: Gray (#888888) — captions, credits
- **Accent primary**: Gold (#c9a96e) — luxury, elegance, used for rules, drop caps, kickers, page numbers
- **Accent secondary**: Bold magenta (#d4376e) — for occasional highlights
- **Cover**: Pure black (#000000) with white text and gold accents

### Typography
- **Body**: Serif (Georgia family), 16-17px, line-height 1.8 — elegant, unhurried
- **Headlines**: High-contrast serif (Didot/Bodoni family — use Georgia or Times as fallback), large (44-56px), often italic, normal weight — thin and elegant, not bold
- **Publication name**: High-contrast serif, enormous (48-64px), all-caps, extremely wide letter-spacing (0.3em) — think VOGUE masthead
- **Subheads**: Sans-serif, uppercase, widely letter-spaced (0.2em), small (12-13px), bold
- **Kickers**: Sans-serif, uppercase, letter-spaced, in gold
- **Byline**: Sans-serif, uppercase, very small (10px), widely letter-spaced
- **Pull quotes**: Serif, italic, large (30-36px), thin/light weight — expressive and delicate

### Layout Elements
- **Drop cap**: 4-5 lines tall, gold, serif — a visual event
- **Pull quote treatment**: Centered, italic serif, thin gold rules (0.5px) above and below — elegant restraint
- **Section dividers**: Short centered gold rules (60px wide, 0.5px) — never full-width
- **Image treatment**: Large and dramatic — full-bleed whenever possible (edge to edge, no margins). Full-page image pages with caption overlaid in white on a dark gradient at bottom.
- **Body layout**: Single column, centered, with very wide margins (20%+ each side). Body text max-width ~520px — mimics narrow columns of fashion magazines.
- **White space**: Extreme. Pages should feel luxuriously empty. If in doubt, leave it out.

### Magazine-Specific Elements
- **Publication name**: Enormous serif at top center of cover — the defining visual element
- **Page numbers**: Serif, italic, small — understated
- **Gutter shadow**: Very subtle
- **Overall feel**: Restrained, confident, every element placed with intention. Photography is king — images get more space than text. Gold as signature accent, used sparingly but consistently.
