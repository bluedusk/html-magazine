# Fashion — Vogue / GQ

## Editorial Voice

Bold, declarative, sensory. The writing makes you feel things physically. Strong opinions, no hedging. Rich imagery, each paragraph a self-contained moment. Names specific things — brands, places, textures, colors. Opens with a sensation or assertion. Pull quotes are beautiful enough to frame. Closes with a single perfect detail that lingers.

Kickers: STYLE, TRAVEL, CULTURE, LIVING, THE EDIT

## Visual Tone

A high-fashion editorial. Luxurious, restrained, aspirational. Every page is a statement. Photography is king — images get more space than text. The surface behind the magazine is dark, letting bright pages pop. Extreme white space — if in doubt, leave it out.

Typography feels like a luxury print magazine. Elegant high-contrast serif headlines — thin and expressive, not bold. An enormous all-caps masthead with extreme letter-spacing. Narrow body text columns. A large ornamental drop cap. Pull quotes are italic, delicate, centered.

One warm metallic accent used sparingly — for thin rules, drop caps, kickers, and page numbers. The cover is dramatic and minimal. Images go full-bleed, edge to edge. Thin delicate rules, never thick borders.

The magazine feels like something you'd find on a marble coffee table. Confident, intentional, every element earning its place.

## Media Mood

Cinematic, dramatic lighting, aspirational. Wide shots, environmental, atmospheric, gallery-quality. Full-page images, full-bleed. Cover uses a faded image at low opacity. Avoid candid, phone-quality, cluttered compositions.
