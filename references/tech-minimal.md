# Tech Minimal Style — Wired / MIT Technology Review

## Editorial Voice

The voice of a publication that lives at the intersection of technology and culture. Sharp, knowing, slightly irreverent.

- **Tone**: Confident, precise, slightly irreverent. Assumes the reader is smart but doesn't assume domain expertise. Explains complex things clearly without dumbing down.
- **Sentence style**: Short-to-medium paragraphs. Mix exposition with data points. Present tense for immediacy. Bold claims backed by specifics.
- **Headlines**: Direct and declarative. Make a statement. "WebAssembly Already Won" over "The Rise of WebAssembly." Can be provocative.
- **Deck**: A sharp follow-up that adds context or a twist. Often starts with a contrasting idea.
- **Lede**: Open with a concrete fact, a surprising number, or a bold claim. No scenic descriptions — get to the point fast.
- **Data**: Numbers are first-class citizens. When content has stats, they deserve visual prominence — large, bold, in their own callout boxes.
- **Quotations**: Brief, attributed with name and title/role. "Solomon Hykes, co-founder of Docker, put it plainly:" — then the quote.
- **Pull quotes**: The most provocative or forward-looking statement. Predictions and bold claims work well.
- **Kickers**: Technical but accessible: "DEEP DIVE", "THE BIG IDEA", "INFRASTRUCTURE", "DATA"
- **Closing**: End with a forward-looking statement or a provocative question. What comes next?

## Visual Elements

### Color & Surface
- **Page background**: Clean white (#ffffff)
- **Surface behind pages**: Near-black (#0a0a0f) — dark, like a monitor turned off
- **Primary text**: Near-black (#111111)
- **Secondary text**: Gray (#777777)
- **Accent primary**: Hot pink/red (#ff0055) — Wired-inspired
- **Accent secondary**: Neon green (#00ff88) — for data highlights, technical elements
- **Accent tertiary**: Electric blue (#0066ff) — for callouts
- **Cover**: Near-black (#0d0d0d) with white text and gradient accent bar

### Typography
- **Body**: Sans-serif (Helvetica Neue family), 16-17px, line-height 1.7, no paragraph indent — use spacing between paragraphs instead
- **Headlines**: Sans-serif, black weight (900), very tight tracking (-0.03em), large (40-52px)
- **Subheads**: Sans-serif, bold, 22-24px, not uppercase — modern and direct
- **Kickers**: Sans-serif, uppercase, bold, very widely letter-spaced (0.3em), small, in accent color
- **Byline**: Sans-serif, uppercase, small, with reading time — elements separated by " // "
- **Pull quotes**: Sans-serif, bold, large, with thick accent-colored left border (4px)
- **Data/stats**: Monospace (Courier New family) — for numbers, metrics, code snippets

### Layout Elements
- **No drop cap**: Instead, oversized bold first line (24px) — drop caps are too traditional for this style
- **Data callout boxes**: Dark background (#111) with large neon green monospace numbers and small sans-serif labels — used for prominent statistics
- **Pull quote treatment**: Left-bordered with accent color, indented from body text
- **Section dividers**: Gradient horizontal rules (3px, linear-gradient from #ff0055 to #0066ff)
- **Image treatment**: Clean, with thin accent-colored border (2-3px) on one side
- **Body layout**: Single column preferred for readability. Occasional two-column for shorter content.
- **Tags**: Monospace tag list on closing page (topic labels)

### Magazine-Specific Elements
- **Publication name**: Bold sans-serif, large, top-left of cover
- **Gradient bar**: 4px gradient stripe (pink → blue) at very top of cover — signature element
- **Issue info**: Monospace, small, at bottom of cover (e.g., "ISSUE 07 // MARCH 2026 // DEEP CIRCUIT")
- **Page numbers**: Monospace, small, in accent color
- **Gutter shadow**: Very subtle — barely there
- **Overall feel**: Sharp, precise, high-contrast. No soft edges, no rounded corners. Geometry and whitespace do the work.

### Photography Direction
- Search terms for Wikimedia: technology, servers, circuits, code, data visualization, abstract digital
- Image mood: Technical, clean, high-contrast. Abstract or close-up preferred over people.
- Treatment: Cover gets a semi-transparent image behind dark overlay. Body images get thin accent border on one side.
