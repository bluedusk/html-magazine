# Business Style — The Economist / Fortune

## Editorial Voice

The voice of a publication that knows more than you do — and will explain it clearly, with occasional dry wit.

- **Tone**: Authoritative, concise, and quietly witty. The Economist is famous for dry humor and understated turns of phrase. Don't be funny — be wry. State facts with quiet confidence.
- **Sentence style**: Dense but clear paragraphs. Every sentence carries information. Use subordinate clauses to pack in context. Paraphrase with attribution rather than quoting at length.
- **Headlines**: Informative with a clever twist. "The Browser's Second Act" — clear topic with a hook. Avoid puns but welcome wordplay that earns its place.
- **Deck**: Sets up the argument. Often a paradox or a "conventional wisdom is wrong" frame.
- **Lede**: Start with the most important fact or the most consequential claim. No narrative warmup. The first sentence should be publishable on its own.
- **Data**: Woven into sentences naturally. "Sales grew 400% in three years" inside a paragraph, not in a callout. Numbers support the argument, they don't replace it.
- **Sidebars**: A hallmark. At least one per article — supplementary data, a definition, a timeline, or a contrasting viewpoint. Sidebars let the main text stay focused while still providing depth.
- **Pull quotes**: The sharpest insight or boldest prediction. Should make the reader want the surrounding context.
- **Kickers**: Categorical and serious: "TECHNOLOGY", "FINANCE", "BRIEFING", "LEADERS", "BUSINESS"
- **Closing**: End with the implication or the "so what." What does this mean for the reader, the industry, the world?

## Visual Elements

### Color & Surface
- **Page background**: Warm cream (#fcfaf5) — Economist-style uncoated paper
- **Surface behind pages**: Charcoal (#34373b)
- **Primary text**: Near-black (#1d1d1d)
- **Secondary text**: Gray (#666666) — captions, metadata
- **Accent primary**: Economist red (#e3120b) — the signature color, used surgically
- **Accent secondary**: Deep navy (#003d6b) — for secondary elements
- **Cover**: Bold red (#e3120b) background — the entire cover is the signature Economist red, white text
- **Sidebar background**: Slightly darker cream (#f0ece3)

### Typography
- **Body**: Serif (Georgia family), 16px, line-height 1.7, paragraph indent (1.2em) — traditional, space-efficient
- **Headlines**: Serif, bold, 34-42px, tight line-height (1.15) — authoritative
- **Subheads**: Sans-serif, bold, uppercase, letter-spaced (0.1em), small (14-16px)
- **Kickers**: Sans-serif, uppercase, bold, small, in red
- **Byline**: Sans-serif, small, gray
- **Pull quotes**: Serif, italic, 24-28px, with red left border (3px solid)
- **Sidebar text**: Sans-serif, smaller (14px), line-height 1.6 — visually distinct from body serif

### Layout Elements
- **Drop cap**: 3 lines tall, red, serif, bold — functional, not decorative
- **Pull quote treatment**: Left-bordered with red, compact — not centered or showy
- **Section dividers**: Thin red rules (1px)
- **Image treatment**: Modest-sized — images inform, they don't dominate. Every image has a caption and credit line.
- **Body layout**: Two-column with column rule — this style is denser than the others, packing more content per page. The Economist signature.
- **Sidebar/callout boxes**: Slightly darker cream background with red left border. Contain facts, definitions, data, context. At least one per article.
- **Takeaway box**: On the closing page — key insight, boxed with red top border

### Magazine-Specific Elements
- **Publication name**: Sans-serif, bold, small, uppercase, letter-spaced at top of cover — dignified, not flashy
- **Cover**: Entire page is red. Publication name and date at top, headline and deck large in white. This is the most distinctive cover of all four styles.
- **Page numbers**: Sans-serif, small, gray
- **Gutter shadow**: Slightly more pronounced — adds to the "thick publication" feel
- **Overall feel**: Authoritative, efficient, trustworthy. Information density that doesn't feel overwhelming, achieved through clear hierarchy and consistent typography. Cream background gives warmth.

### Photography Direction
- Search terms for Wikimedia: [topic] business, industry, economics, infrastructure, institutions
- Image mood: Informational, documentary, factual. Not artistic — images serve the story.
- Treatment: Modestly sized, always captioned. Never full-bleed — this isn't a fashion magazine.
