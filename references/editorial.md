# Editorial — NYT Magazine / The Atlantic

## Editorial Voice

Measured, literary, unhurried. Assumes an intelligent reader. Long sentences mixed with short punchy ones. Opens with a scene or detail, never throat-clears. Headlines are poetic and intriguing. Pull quotes make you stop and think. Closes with an image, not a summary.

Kickers: ESSAY, DISPATCH, CULTURE, IDEAS

## Visual Tone

A premium Sunday supplement. Serious, refined, immersive. Pages feel like quality uncoated paper — warm, not stark white. The surface behind the magazine is dark, like a wooden desk. Generous white space everywhere. Pages should never feel crowded.

### Typography Character
Body text is serif — elegant, bookish, comfortable for long reading. Headlines are serif, bold, with tight letter-spacing — authoritative but not loud. Subheads are sans-serif, uppercase, small, widely letter-spaced — quiet signposts. Kickers are small, uppercase, in the accent color. Byline uses small caps. Drop cap on the opening paragraph — large, in the accent color.

### Color Mood
Warm off-white pages. Dark, moody cover with high-contrast text. A single strong accent color (deep red or burgundy family) used sparingly — for kickers, drop caps, pull quote marks, and thin dividing rules.

### Layout Character
Opening pages use single column with very generous margins. Body pages can use two-column with a subtle column rule for denser content. Pull quotes are centered with thin accent-colored rules above and below, with large faded decorative quotation marks. Thin horizontal rules in the accent color separate sections. Images occasionally bleed to page edges, otherwise sit with italic serif captions.

## Media Mood
Documentary, atmospheric, natural light. Moody, thoughtful, nothing posed or cheerful. Large hero on cover with dark overlay. Body images with serif italic captions.
