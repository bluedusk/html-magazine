# Editorial Style — NYT Magazine / The Atlantic

## Editorial Voice

The writing of a serious literary magazine. Every sentence is measured, every word chosen with care.

- **Tone**: Thoughtful, unhurried, confident without being showy. Assumes an intelligent reader who doesn't need things spelled out.
- **Sentence style**: Long, carefully constructed sentences mixed with short punchy ones. Subordinate clauses that add nuance. Paragraphs breathe — they don't rush.
- **Headlines**: Evocative, often poetic. Prefer intrigue over information. "The Silence Between the Signals" over "How AI Is Changing Everything."
- **Deck**: One elegant sentence that deepens the headline, never repeats it.
- **Lede**: Open with a scene, a specific detail, or a quietly surprising observation. Never throat-clear ("In today's rapidly changing world...").
- **Quotations**: Woven into the narrative, not dumped in. Paraphrase with attribution when the source's exact words aren't remarkable.
- **Pull quotes**: The sentence that makes someone stop and think — insight over shock value.
- **Kickers**: Understated: "ESSAY", "DISPATCH", "CULTURE", "IDEAS"
- **Closing**: End with an image, not a summary. Leave the reader with something to sit with.

## Visual Elements

These are the concrete visual characteristics to pass to the rendering engine:

### Color & Surface
- **Page background**: Warm off-white (#faf9f6) — like quality uncoated paper stock
- **Surface behind pages**: Dark charcoal (#2c2c2c) — a wooden desk or dark table
- **Primary text**: Near-black (#1a1a1a)
- **Secondary text**: Medium gray (#666666) — captions, bylines, dates
- **Accent color**: Deep crimson (#c0392b) — used sparingly for kickers, drop caps, rules, pull quote marks
- **Cover**: Dark moody gradient (#1a1a2e → #16213e) with white text

### Typography
- **Body**: Serif (Georgia family), 17-18px, line-height 1.75, paragraph indent on continuation paragraphs
- **Headlines**: Serif, bold, tight letter-spacing (-0.02em), large
- **Subheads**: Sans-serif, bold, uppercase, widely letter-spaced, small (13-14px)
- **Kickers**: Sans-serif, uppercase, letter-spaced, in accent color
- **Byline**: Sans-serif, small caps
- **Pull quotes**: Serif, italic, large (28-32px), with oversized decorative quotation marks in faded accent color

### Layout Elements
- **Drop cap**: 3-4 lines tall, accent color, serif font on the opening paragraph
- **Pull quote treatment**: Centered, bordered top and bottom with thin accent-colored rules, large faded quotation mark as decorative element
- **Section dividers**: Thin horizontal rules (1px) in accent color
- **Image treatment**: Occasional full-bleed (edge to edge), otherwise with italic serif captions below
- **Body layout**: Single column with generous margins (15-20% each side) for opening pages; two-column with column rule for denser body pages
- **Sidebar/callout**: None typically — this style prefers clean, uncluttered pages

### Magazine-Specific Elements
- **Publication name**: Small, elegant, sans-serif, uppercase, widely letter-spaced at the top of the cover
- **Page numbers**: Sans-serif, 10px, centered at bottom
- **Gutter shadow**: Subtle inset shadow on binding edge
- **Paper texture**: Very subtle noise pattern on page background
- **Overall feel**: Serious, refined, immersive. Generous white space. Pages should never feel crowded.

### Photography Direction
- Search terms for Wikimedia: [topic]-related, prefer documentary/editorial photography, avoid stock-photo aesthetics
- Image mood: Moody, natural lighting, candid or environmental, not posed
- Treatment: Large hero on cover (with dark overlay for text readability), modest-sized images in body with serif italic captions
