# Creator — Dazed / i-D / The Face

## Editorial Voice

Casual, authentic, internet-native. Reads like your coolest friend telling you something they just discovered. First-person friendly. Slang and grammar-breaking on purpose. Short punchy sentences. Fragments are fine. ALL CAPS for emphasis. Opens with a hot take or personal moment. Pull quotes are the most shareable line — the sentence someone would screenshot. Closes on an emotional beat.

Kickers: MOOD, REAL TALK, MAIN CHARACTER, IYKYK, THE MOMENT, CREATOR

## Visual Tone

A youth culture magazine that feels alive. Energetic, messy-on-purpose, like scrolling through a well-curated creative feed but on paper. This is the opposite of the other three styles — bright, not dark. The surface behind the magazine is light, colorful, or gradient. Not moody.

Typography is bold and playful. Chunky oversized sans-serif headlines that can be tilted, stacked, or overlapping. Mix sizes within a headline. Casual body text with lots of breathing room. Kickers styled as small rounded pill tags, like social media labels. No drop cap — too formal. First word or sentence is oversized instead.

One bold pop color per magazine — electric, neon, punchy. Used everywhere without restraint. Rounded corners on everything — images, cards, tags, callouts. Elements can overlap. Layout is asymmetric, broken grid, deliberately uneven. Sticker-like labels scattered on pages.

The magazine feels young, authentic, and energetic. Like something a creator would share in their story.

## Media Mood

Candid, behind-the-scenes, authentic. Phone-quality is fine. Portraits with character, street photography, flat lays, lifestyle moments. Images get rounded corners, can be cutouts on colored backgrounds. Avoid stock photography, corporate aesthetics, dark/moody vibes.
